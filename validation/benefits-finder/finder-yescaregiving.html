1. Income Programs

  • Caregiver benefits
    (Government of Canada)

    Caregiver benefits allow eligible caregivers to receive up to 35 weeks of financial assistance to provide care or support to a critically ill or injured person. Caregivers must be family members or someone who is considered to be like family to the person needing care or support.

  • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Children's Benefits
    (Government of Canada)

    Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Children's Benefits provide a monthly benefit to the dependent children of disabled or deceased contributors.

    Application

    • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Children's Benefits - Form

    Additional Information

    • You must have a Social Insurance Number
  • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Death Benefit
    (Government of Canada)

    The Death Benefit Pension Plan (CPP) is a single amount payable to the estate of a deceased contributor or on behalf of the estate.

    Application

    • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Death Benefit - Form

    Additional Information

    • Apply as soon as possible after contributor's death as CPP can only make back payments for up to twelve months.
  • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Retirement Pension
    (Government of Canada)

    The Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Retirement Pension provides a monthly taxable benefit to retired contributors.

    Application

    • Canada Pension Plan (CPP) Retirement Pension - Form
  • Guaranteed Income Supplement (GIS)
    (Government of Canada)

    This program provides a monthly non-taxable benefit to low-income Old Age Security recipients.

    To be eligible, you must also be receiving

    • Old Age Security (OAS)
  • Lived or Living Outside Canada - Pensions and Benefits
    (Government of Canada)

    The International Benefits program may provide retirement, disability or survivor benefits to eligible individuals who have lived or worked in another country, or the surviving spouse, common-law partner or children of eligible individuals who have lived or worked in another country.

  • Old Age Security (OAS)
    (Government of Canada)

    This pension provides a monthly taxable benefit to persons 65 years of age and over.

    Application

    • Old Age Security (OAS) - Form

2. Other Programs

  • Goods and Services Tax/Harmonized Sales Tax (GST/HST) Credit
    (Government of Canada)

    This program issues full and partial goods and services tax credits to Canadians with low incomes and issues full and partial rebates on the federal portion of the harmonized sales tax and issues some related provincial payments.
