5. After you’ve applied

After you complete the application for the Benefit, you must submit:

  • the Medical Certificate for Employment Insurance Family Caregiver Benefits form
  • the Authorization to Release a Medical Certificate for Employment Insurance Family Caregiver Benefits form
  • all your Records of Employment (ROEs) for the past 52 weeks

Important:

If you are not related to the critically ill or injured child by blood, marriage, common-law partnership, or any legal parent-child relationship, but are considered to be like family, you must submit a Family Member Attestation form.

You can mail these documents to Service Canada at the address provided on the confirmation page when you complete your online application, or take them to your local Service Canada Centre.

Medical certificate

The Medical Certificate for Employment Insurance Family Caregiver Benefits form must be completed and signed by a medical doctor or nurse practitioner. This certificate:

  • confirms that the child is critically ill or injured
  • confirms that the child needs the care or support of one or more caregivers, and
  • provides the specific time period for which the critically ill or injured child is expected to need care or support

The number of weeks you are entitled to receive the Benefit and the start date of the Benefit are based on the information provided on the medical certificate. If the critically ill or injured adult needs care or support for a longer period of time than stated on the medical certificate, you will need a second certificate from a medical doctor or nurse practitioner to extend the original time period.

Authorization to release a medical certificate

Doctors and nurse practitioners are not allowed to release medical information about a patient without the patient’s consent. Therefore, the critically ill or injured child’s parent or legal guardian must fill out an Authorization to Release a Medical Certificate  form to authorize the release of medical information. This must be submitted to Service Canada along with the medical certificate.

Record of Employment

If your employer submits ROEs to Service Canada electronically, you do not need to provide a copy after your application is complete.

If your employer issues ROEs in paper format , you must request a copy of all the ROEs issued to you during the past 52 weeks and submit the original ROEs to Service Canada.

Family member attestation

If the critically ill or injured child is not related by blood, marriage, common-law partnership, or any legal parent-child relationship, but considers you to be like family, you must confirm this by submitting a Family Member Attestation form. It must be signed by the critically ill or injured child's parent or legal guardian.

What happens after I apply

Service Canada will mail you a benefit statement once your application is complete. This statement will include a 4-digit access code. You will need this code and your SIN to inquire about your application. Receiving the EI benefit statement does not mean that Service Canada has made a decision about your claim.

To check the progress of your application, you can contact Service Canada or log in to My Service Canada Account. If you do not have an account, you can register for one once you receive your access code.

What do I have to do while I am receiving the Benefit

While you are receiving the Benefit, you must continue to be eligible. Please contact Service Canada in the following situations:

  • You have earnings
  • The child you are providing care or support to no longer requires your care or support
  • To confirm which caregiver has served the one-week waiting period if you are sharing the Benefit with other caregivers for the same critically ill or injured adult

If you do not inform Service Canada of these changes, you risk being overpaid and having to repay benefits.

What can I do if I disagree with the decision on my application

You can request a reconsideration of the decision. You must submit a request for reconsideration within 30 days of the date of the decision.
