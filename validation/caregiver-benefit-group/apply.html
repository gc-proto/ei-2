Supporting documents you must submit after you finish applying online

Do not wait until you have all of these documents before you apply online. You can collect and send them after you complete your online application.

Medical certificate

For a critically ill or injured child or adult

Medical Certificate for Employment Insurance Family Caregiver Benefits form: a medical doctor or nurse practitioner must complete and sign this form.

This certificate:

  • confirms that the person is critically ill or injured
  • confirms that the person needs the care or support of one or more caregivers
  • provides the specific time period for which the critically ill or injured person is expected to need care or support

The information provided on the medical certificate determines:

  • the start date of the benefit
  • the number of weeks you are entitled to receive the benefit

If the critically ill or injured person needs care or support for a longer period of time than stated on the medical certificate, you will need a second certificate from a medical doctor or nurse practitioner to extend the original time period.

For a loved one needing end-of-life care

Medical Certificate for Employment Insurance Compassionate Care Benefits form: a medical doctor or nurse practitioner must complete and sign this form.

This certificate confirms the gravely ill person is at significant risk of death within 26 weeks.

Only one medical certificate is required within the 52-week period. This applies if only one person is claiming the 26 weeks of benefits, or if the benefit is being shared. If you submit more than one medical certificate, the first certificate determines the start and end dates of the 52-week period.

Authorization to release a medical certificate

Doctors and nurse practitioners are not allowed to release medical information about a patient without the patient’s consent.

Authorization to Release a Medical Certificate: the critically ill or injured person, or their legal representative, must complete this form to authorize the release of medical information.

In the case of a critically ill or injured child, their parent or legal guardian must complete the form.

The form must be submitted to Service Canada at the same time as the medical certificate.

Record of Employment

If your employer submits ROEs to Service Canada electronically, you do not need to provide a copy after your application is complete.

If your employer issues ROEs in paper format, you must request a copy of all the ROEs issued to you during the past 52 weeks and submit the original ROEs to Service Canada.

More information about ROEs

Attestation for non-family member

You can receive caregiver benefits to care for a loved one who considers you a family member, such as a close friend or neighbour.

Family Member Attestation form: the critically ill or injured person or their legal representative must complete this form to appoint you as a caregiver. For a child, the parent or legal guardian must sign the form.

Personal information you will need to apply online

Make sure you have all the information listed below:

  • the names and addresses of your employers for the last 52 weeks
  • the dates you were employed with each employer, and the reasons you are no longer employed with them
  • your detailed explanation of the facts, if you quit or have been dismissed from any job in the last 52 weeks
  • your full mailing address (and your home address, if they are not the same)
  • your Social Insurance Number (SIN)
  • your mother’s maiden name
  • information about the critically ill or injured child, including full name, date of birth and home address
  • if you want to sign up for direct deposit: your banking information, including financial institution, branch (transit) number and your account number

Complete the online application

The online application takes about an hour to complete.

Consult the Help instructions on each page of the application if you have questions.

Information saved for 72 hours

Your information is saved for 72 hours (3 days) from the time you start your application. If you cannot complete the application all at once, you can come back to it later using a temporary password that is generated when you start your application. If you do not complete the application within 72 hours, it will be deleted and you will have to start a new application.

Apply online now

Submit supporting documents

After you complete your online application, mail your supporting documents to Service Canada (the address is also provided on the confirmation page), or take them to your local Service Canada Centre.

Wait for your benefit statement and access code

Service Canada mails you a benefit statement once your application is complete. This statement includes a 4-digit access code. You need this code and your SIN to inquire about your application. Receiving the EI benefit statement does not mean that Service Canada has made a decision about your claim.

If you have received any EI benefit in the past, you will not receive a new access code. You can continue to use the same access code that you already had.

Check the status of your application

To check the progress of your application, you can contact Service Canada or log in to My Service Canada Account. If you do not have an account, you can register for one once you receive your access code.
